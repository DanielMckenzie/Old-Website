About me.

I am a third year graduate student at the University of Georgia . On the right is a photo of me and my dad; I am the one on the left.

On the 6th of June 2016 i passed my oral exam and was admitted to candidacy. You can find my slides in the 'notes' section of this page.

Before coming to Georgia I was an undergraduate at University of Cape Town from where I also received my Master's degree.

In between Cape Town and Athens, Georgia I spent a semester at the University of Bayreuth in Germany, working with Professor Fabrizio Catanese and his Lehrstuhl.

You can find a copy of my professional resume here .

Research Interests.

I am primarily interested in applied and applicable math, and my advisor is Dr. Ming-Jun Lai

I am currently working on an approach to spectral clustering for graphs that utilizes some ideas from Compressive Sensing.

I have also thought about wavelets and multiscale dictionary learning lately.

In a previous life I worked in pure mathematics, specifically algebraic and differential geometry.

Teaching.

Here at UGA I have had the privilege of teaching Calculus and Pre-Calculus

For the last two semesters I have been a TA for an experimental online Pre-Calculus course run through the University System of Georgia over several campuses

This summer (2016) I am again the TA for online Pre-Calculus, and will be involved in adapting the course to the UGA summer schedule.
